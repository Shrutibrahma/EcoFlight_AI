01 — The Problem

Why Standard Routing Falls Short

Commercial aviation accounts for roughly 2.5% of global CO₂ emissions — but when non-CO₂ effects like contrail formation and NOₓ are factored in, aviation's effective climate forcing is estimated at 3.5× its CO₂ contribution alone (Lee et al., 2021). Traditional Flight Management Systems optimize for a single metric: minimum fuel burn. They ignore atmospheric conditions that cause persistent contrails, fail to exploit favorable wind structures dynamically, and treat time, fuel, and emissions as independent objectives rather than a unified trade-off surface.

EcoFlight AI re-frames routing as a multi-objective, physics-aware optimization problem — simultaneously minimizing fuel consumption, CO₂-equivalent emissions, and contrail formation while respecting real-world airspace constraints.

03 — Physics Foundation

The Equations Behind the Routes

Range & Fuel — Breguet Equation

The fundamental physics constraint on fuel burn per segment comes from the Breguet Range Equation. The RL agent embeds this as a hard differentiable constraint layer — no predicted route can violate the laws of thermodynamics.

R  =  V · (L/D) SFC  ·  ln Wi Wi − ΔWf
RRange of segment (km)
VTrue airspeed (m/s)
L/DLift-to-drag ratio
SFCSpecific fuel consumption (kg/N·s)
WiAircraft weight at segment start
ΔWfFuel burned in segment
Breguet, L. (1922). Calcul du poids de combustible consommé par un avion en vol ascendant. Comptes Rendus de l'Académie des Sciences, 177. Standard formulation per Raymer (1992) and Jenkinson et al. (1999).

Multi-Objective Cost Function

Each graph edge (waypoint-to-waypoint segment) is assigned a cost that the optimizer minimizes. Our cost function is a weighted linear scalarization across three objectives:

C(e) = wf · F(e) + wc · CO₂eq(e) + wt · T(e)
wf — fuel wc — climate wt — time

wf + wc + wt = 1

CO₂eq contrail term: Teoh et al. (2022), Mitigating the Climate Forcing of Aircraft Contrails. Geophysical Research Letters, 49(3). Scalarization framework: Daulton et al. (2020), NeurIPS.

F(e) is computed via the Breguet equation per segment. CO₂eq(e) combines direct CO₂ mass with an ISSR humidity factor for contrail-formation risk using Effective Radiative Forcing weighting. T(e) is segment time adjusted for wind component. Weights are tunable — operators trace the Pareto frontier between cost and climate benefit.

04 — Algorithm

How We Find the Optimal Path

Physics-Informed A* with RL Heuristic

Standard pathfinding algorithms were not designed for atmospheric routing complexity. EcoFlight AI uses a modified A* search with a learned heuristic function — replacing the traditional geometric distance heuristic with a neural network trained via reinforcement learning on millions of historical flight segments.

Optimization Pipeline

1
Graph Construction Airspace discretized into a 3D grid of waypoints. Edges weighted by C(e) using live weather and Breguet physics.
2
RL Agent Heuristic A deep Q-network trained on historical routes provides h(n) — an admissible estimate of remaining cost from any node to destination.
3
Physics-Informed NN Layer Breguet constraint layer ensures every predicted fuel burn satisfies aerodynamic physics. No physically impossible route can be selected.
4
Multi-Objective Optimizer Pareto-optimal front computed across fuel, CO₂eq, and time — operator selects preferred trade-off point.
5
Continuous Learning Loop Each completed flight feeds actual fuel and contrail data back to retrain the RL agent — accuracy compounds over time.
